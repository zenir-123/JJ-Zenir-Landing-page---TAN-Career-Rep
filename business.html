01 · Ownership Continuity

Buy-Sell Agreement Funding

A buy-sell agreement decides what happens to an owner's share of the business if they die, become disabled, or leave. Life insurance funds it — so the remaining owners have the cash to buy out that share without draining the company or taking on debt.

Typically a good fit for

  • Partnerships and multi-owner LLCs or S-corps
  • Family businesses where some heirs are in and some are out
  • Any co-owned company without a written exit plan

Worth knowing: without funding, a buy-sell agreement is just a promise — the money has to come from somewhere on the worst day of the company's life.

How I Can Help

  • Coordinate with your attorney and CPA — they draft the agreement, I make sure it's funded
  • Compare funding structures, like cross-purchase versus entity-owned, in plain terms
  • Size coverage to your actual valuation, not a guess from three years ago
  • Review it as the business grows so the funding keeps pace with what it's worth

02 · Revenue Protection

Key Person Insurance

Key person insurance is a policy the business owns on someone it can't afford to lose — a founder, a top producer, the one person who holds the client relationships. If they pass away, the business receives the payout to cover lost revenue, recruit a replacement, and steady lenders and customers.

Typically a good fit for

  • Companies where revenue depends heavily on one or two people
  • Businesses with loans or investors that require it
  • Small teams without a deep bench behind critical roles

How I Can Help

  • Identify who's actually key and put a real number on what losing them would cost
  • Compare term and permanent structures so you're not overpaying for the protection you need
  • Set up ownership and beneficiary details correctly — small errors here have big tax consequences
  • Review annually as roles, revenue, and people change

03 · Your Team

Group Benefits

Group benefits are coverage you offer your team — group life and supplemental health options that make your compensation more than a paycheck. For a small business, they're one of the most affordable ways to compete for people who have other offers.

Typically a good fit for

  • Owners losing good people to bigger companies' benefits
  • Growing teams with no formal benefits package yet
  • Businesses that want to add value without a huge fixed cost

How I Can Help

  • Assess options that fit your headcount and budget — not an enterprise package scaled down badly
  • Explain employer-paid versus voluntary so you know exactly what each costs you
  • Handle enrollment and employee questions so it doesn't land on your desk
  • Re-shop the coverage periodically to keep it competitive as you grow
