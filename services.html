01 · Most Popular

Term Life Insurance

Term life covers you for a set window — usually 10, 20, or 30 years — and pays your family a tax-free benefit if you pass away during that time. It's the simplest, most affordable way to put a large amount of protection in place.

Typically a good fit for

  • Parents with kids still at home
  • Homeowners with a mortgage to protect
  • Anyone whose income someone else depends on
  • People who want real coverage on a real budget

Worth knowing: term rates are locked to your age and health on the day you apply — coverage never gets cheaper than it is today.

How I Can Help

  • Compare carriers side by side — I'm not limited to quoting one company's rates
  • Right-size your coverage to your income, mortgage, and your kids' timeline — not a rounded guess
  • Explain riders in plain English, like living benefits and the option to convert to permanent coverage later
  • Review your policy yearly so it keeps up as life changes

02 · Grow While Protected

Indexed Universal Life (IUL)

IUL is permanent coverage that also builds cash value. That value grows with the performance of a market index — with a floor, so a bad market year doesn't take money away — and you can adjust your premiums as life changes.

Typically a good fit for

  • People who want coverage that lasts a lifetime, not a term
  • Savers looking for tax-advantaged growth alongside protection
  • Higher earners who've maxed out other retirement options
  • Anyone who values flexibility over a rigid premium schedule

How I Can Help

  • Run real illustrations with conservative assumptions — not best-case sales math
  • Explain caps, floors, and participation rates before you commit, so there are no surprises
  • Compare IUL designs across carriers to find the structure that fits your goals
  • Review funding regularly so the policy stays healthy for the long haul

03 · Peace of Mind

Final Expense Insurance

Final expense is a smaller whole life policy — typically $5,000 to $50,000 — built to cover funeral costs, medical bills, and other end-of-life expenses so your family is never left with the bill. Approval is simple, often with no medical exam.

Typically a good fit for

  • Seniors who want simple, affordable protection
  • People with health conditions who've been declined elsewhere
  • Anyone who wants funeral costs handled ahead of time

How I Can Help

  • Match you to carriers that fit your health history, including simplified and guaranteed-issue options
  • Size the policy against real costs, not a round number pulled from the air
  • Lock in a premium that never increases and coverage that never expires
  • Make sure your family knows the policy exists and exactly how to use it
